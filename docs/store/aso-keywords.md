## Methodology

Keyword selection is based on:
1. Competitor App Store keyword inference (title + subtitle + visible copy analysis)
2. Search intent alignment with Travel Checker's three core differentiators:
   interactive map, budget tracking, trip journaling
3. Volume/competition balance — high-volume terms with established incumbents are included
   only when Travel Checker has a genuine content angle to rank for
4. Negative keyword logic — terms that imply features Travel Checker does NOT have
   (e.g., "booking," "planner," "itinerary") are excluded to avoid irrelevant installs
   and poor conversion rates
5. App name and subtitle terms are NOT repeated in the keyword field (Apple ignores duplicates)

### Keyword-by-keyword rationale

| Keyword | Search Intent | Competition | Volume Est. | Include |
|---------|--------------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| travel journal | Users want a diary/log of past trips | Medium | High | YES |
| trip tracker | Tracking past trips, not planning | Medium | High | YES |
| world map | Visualizing travel on a map | Medium | High | YES |
| budget travel | Cost-conscious travelers, budget breakdown | Low-Medium | Medium | YES |
| travel diary | Journaling angle, overlaps with "travel journal" | Low | Medium | YES |
| trip log | Simpler synonym for trip tracker | Low | Medium | YES |
| countries visited | Highly specific to our stats feature | Low | Medium | YES |
| travel app | Generic catch-all, broad intent | High | High | YES (last priority) |

### Terms excluded and why

| Keyword Excluded | Reason |
|-----------------|--------|
| trip planner | Travel Checker is a log, not a planner — wrong intent, low conversion |
| flight tracker | Not a flight or itinerary tool |
| hotel booking | No booking features in v1 |
| itinerary | Business travel connotation, TripIt dominates this term |
| travel guide | Implies curated recommendations, not user-generated logs |
| polarsteps | Competitor brand keyword — Apple prohibits competitor brand terms in keyword field |
| travel tracker | Duplicate concept of "trip tracker" — less natural phrasing |
| solo travel | Too broad, not keyword-matched to a feature |
| map app | Intent is navigation (Google Maps, Apple Maps), not travel log |
| photo album | Generic, competed by Photos app, low travel intent |

## Competitor App Store Presence Analysis

Understanding how competitors use their keyword fields informs our own strategy:
where they are strong (avoid direct head-to-head), where they are weak (opportunity).

### Polarsteps
- **App Store title:** "Polarsteps — Travel Tracker"
- **Keyword strengths:** "trip tracker," "travel tracker," "route tracking," "auto track"
- **Keyword gaps:** "budget," "expense," "cost," "social," "travel journal," "diary"
- **Our opportunity:** Own the budget + journal intersection that they ignore entirely.

### Visited
- **App Store title:** "Visited — Countries Visited Map"
- **Keyword strengths:** "countries visited," "scratch map," "travel map," "visited countries"
- **Keyword gaps:** "trip," "journal," "budget," "photos," "destination," "diary"
- **Our opportunity:** "countries visited" is partially owned by Visited but we can compete on
  the combination "countries visited + journal + budget" which they cannot match.

### TripIt
- **App Store title:** "TripIt — Travel Organizer"
- **Keyword strengths:** "itinerary," "trip organizer," "flight," "business travel"
- **Keyword gaps:** "map," "budget," "journal," "social," "photos," "memories"
- **Our opportunity:** Everything outside the itinerary/business travel box. Do not
  target "itinerary" — let TripIt own it and focus on where they have zero presence.

### Wanderlog
- **App Store title:** "Wanderlog: Trip Planner & Map"
- **Keyword strengths:** "trip planner," "travel planner," "map," "road trip"
- **Keyword gaps:** "budget tracking," "expense," "journal," "diary," "memories," "social"
- **Our opportunity:** Wanderlog owns "planner" — we own "log/journal/diary."
  Avoid "planner" terms entirely; reinforce "journal" and "log" positioning.

### Been
- **App Store title:** "Been — Countries Visited"
- **Keyword strengths:** "countries visited," "scratch map," "map tracker"
- **Keyword gaps:** Everything. Minimal feature set means minimal keyword coverage.
- **Our opportunity:** Compete directly on "countries visited" — we offer the same
  feature plus photos, budget, journal, and social. Better product, should win over time.

## Google Play Keyword Strategy

Google Play does not have a dedicated keyword field like Apple. Instead, keywords must
be embedded organically in the title, short description, and full description.
Google's algorithm indexes and ranks the full text.

### Keyword density targets for the full description

The following terms should appear naturally in the full description, each at least once
(ideally 2-3 times for primary terms without keyword stuffing):

| Term | Target occurrences | Placement priority |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| travel journal | 3 | Title, opening paragraph, feature header |
| trip tracker / log trips | 2 | Short description, body |
| world map | 3 | Opening paragraph, map feature section, closing |
| budget tracking / track budgets | 3 | Short description, budget section, comparison section |
| photos | 2 | Journey logging section, body |
| countries visited | 2 | Statistics section, body |
| social | 2 | Community section |
| GDPR | 1 | Privacy section — signals trustworthiness to EU users |
| free | 2 | "What is free" section + short description |

### Google Play Category Tags

Primary: Travel & Local
Secondary tags (select all that apply in Play Console): Travel, Journal, Lifestyle, Map

## Localization Priorities

### Tier 1 — Launch markets (English, immediate)

- **US (en-US):** Largest iOS market. Primary focus for launch. Current copy is already
  optimized for US English.
- **UK (en-GB):** Second English-language market. Same copy works; minor tweaks:
  "travelling" (double-l), "colour" — but not required. US English is accepted.
- **Australia (en-AU):** Strong travel culture, high iOS penetration. Same copy.

### Tier 2 — Localize within 90 days of launch

These markets have large travel app user bases, high iOS share, and strong travel culture.
ROI on localization is high because the user base is motivated and App Store search is
largely language-gated.

#### German (de-DE)

- **Market rationale:** Germany is the largest travel market in Europe by spend.
  Germans are among the world's most active international travelers.
- **Key terms to research:** "Reisetagebuch" (travel diary), "Reise-App" (travel app),
  "Reisekosten" (travel costs), "besuchte Länder" (countries visited), "Reisekarte" (travel map)
- **Note:** German App Store searches are predominantly in German, not English. Not
  localizing means missing a significant portion of impressionable organic traffic.

#### French (fr-FR)

- **Market rationale:** France is the world's most visited country. French travelers
  are active app users. Strong iOS penetration in urban demographics.
- **Key terms:** "journal de voyage" (travel journal), "carnet de voyage" (travel log),
  "budget voyage" (travel budget), "carte du monde" (world map), "pays visités"

#### Spanish (es-ES / es-MX)

- **Market rationale:** Two distinct markets — Spain (EU GDPR, high purchasing power)
  and Mexico/Latin America (high volume, lower purchasing power). Prioritize es-ES.
- **Key terms:** "diario de viaje" (travel diary), "rastreador de viajes" (trip tracker),
  "mapa mundial" (world map), "países visitados" (countries visited), "gastos de viaje"

#### Italian (it-IT)

- **Market rationale:** Italy has strong travel culture and high iPhone penetration
  (Italy is above EU average for iPhone market share). Travel journaling has cultural resonance.
- **Key terms:** "diario di viaggio," "mappa del mondo," "paesi visitati," "spese di viaggio"

### Tier 3 — Localize in 6-12 months (data-dependent)

Proceed only if Tier 1/2 markets show organic search traffic from these regions in
App Store Connect analytics.

| Market | Language | Rationale |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Brazil | pt-BR | Large iOS base, active travel community, growing outbound travel |
| Netherlands | nl-NL | High travel spend per capita, strong digital nomad culture |
| Scandinavia (combined) | no / sv / da | High purchasing power, strong travel culture, very high iOS share |
| Poland | pl-PL | Fast-growing travel market, budget-conscious travelers — strong fit |
| Japan | ja-JP | High iPhone share, travel documentation culture (hobonichi, techo) |

### Localization notes

1. Do NOT machine-translate and publish directly. Have a native speaker review
   all localized listings before submission, especially keywords and short description.
2. App Store keywords are language-specific — a German keyword field must contain
   German keywords, not translated English keywords.
3. Screenshots can often remain in English with translated caption overlays for Tier 2
   markets. Full UI localization is not required for the store listing.
4. Price localization: Apple handles currency conversion automatically, but consider
   setting manual prices for major markets when in-app purchases are introduced in v1.1.

## Seasonal Keyword Opportunties

App Store and Google Play search volume for travel-related terms spikes predictably.
Adjust promotional text (iOS) and short description (Android) before these windows:

| Period | Spike reason | Temporary keyword/copy angle |
|--------|-------------|------------------------------|
| December 15 - January 10 | Year-in-review behavior; new year goals | "Review your year of travel," "How many countries did you visit this year?" |
| February 1-28 | Valentine's Day + winter escape planning | "Couple travel journal," "Plan your escape, remember it forever" |
| May 15 - June 30 | Summer trip planning begins | "Summer travel journal," "Track your summer adventures" |
| August 1-31 | Peak summer travel | "Log your summer trip," "Summer memories, all in one place" |
| October 1-15 | Autumn travel + post-summer recap | "Summer recap," "How much did your trips cost this year?" |

**Implementation:** Use Apple's Promotional Text field (can be updated without app review)
and Google Play's "What's New" section for seasonal copy changes.
